Space 001

Description

  1. Living room/kitchen not connected to other spaces
  2. Window-to-Wall Area Ratio 25-35%
  3. Ceiling Height > 3.8 m
  4. Common residential rectangle
  5. Exterior wall total area: 170 m2
  6. Window area: 47.505 m2
  7. Window-to-wall ratio: 28%
The user configuration is set for a small family of three individuals. The couple is between 35 and 45 years old, and the child is around 9 years old.

The main living spaces include the master bedroom with a basic bathroom, a guest room, a study (also serving as a guest room), a kitchen, and a living room, each functioning as independent areas with a complete foyer.

The overall layout is square-shaped, with ample natural light in the living room and balcony. The kitchen has a convenient work balcony for storage, and the spatial transitions are gently buffered, providing a comfortable atmosphere. The ceiling height of the space is 3.85 meters.

Foyer


Utilizing a stable wood grain color paired with black-based cabinets, the lower section is designed as open shelves for easy access to everyday shoes without the need to frequently open or close doors. The back wall of the shoe-wearing chair features a wooden lattice for hanging clothes and a design that serves as a coat rack. This section is ideal for hanging everyday outerwear, bags, and hats. The opposite wall uses a special hand-applied gray texture paint combined with a large floor-to-ceiling gray mirror, not only enhancing the sense of space but also serving as a dressing mirror.
Matte Gray Baking Paint / Matte Black Baking Paint / Wood Veneer / Special Paint with Hand-Applied Clear Water Model in Gray / Matte Black Hardware Handles / Gray Mirror / Wood Flooring / Recessed Lighting

Kitchen


Due to the ample space available, a kitchen island has been incorporated to enhance the usable countertop area. On the other side, a dining sideboard is placed to increase the kitchen's storage functionality. The dining table features a curved tabletop, sleek iron table legs, and refined pendant lights, elevating the overall aesthetic. The kitchen utilizes a black marble countertop to complement the gray cabinets and some wooden veneer.
Matte Gray Baking Paint / Black Marble / White Paint / Wood Veneer / Wood Flooring / Recessed Lighting

Living Room


Similarly adhering to a color scheme of black, gray, and white, wood grain is introduced to add warmth to the space and prevent it from feeling too cold. The wood grain extends directly to the bottom of the corridor, and the sofa wall is adorned with special paint and wooden lattice, echoing the design of the foyer for a unified feel. The TV wall features a combination of low cabinets and tall cabinets, incorporating a misty fireplace, instantly elevating the warm ambiance of the space. To enhance design vibrancy, the back wall of the TV section introduces segmented lines, cut in proportion for added visual interest.
Matte White Baking Paint / White Paint / Matte Gray Baking Paint / Special Paint with Hand-Applied Clear Water Model in Gray / Wood Veneer / Wood Flooring / Recessed Lighting

Study


The desk extends forward, creating a sense of spaciousness and sophistication. Narrow-framed black cabinets add an elegant quality to the space, complemented by the wood grain extending along the back of the owner's chair. The wall features segmented lines and a varied display bookshelf, maintaining a balanced and interesting atmosphere in the space.
Matte Iron Gray Baking Paint / Wood Veneer / White Paint / Wood Flooring / Recessed Lighting

Secondary Bedroom


In terms of layout, opting for a bed with a tatami mattress is chosen to create a sense of increased space and to enhance storage functionality. A full wall of wardrobe storage cabinets, using a combination of wood grain and gray tones with contrasting accents, serves both practical and aesthetic purposes. The gray cabinet doors employ varying sizes of segmentation to introduce an element of visual interest. Similarly, the bookshelf on the other side employs an uneven sizing approach to proportionally divide the space, combining open and closed sections to enhance spatial appeal.
Matte Gray Baking Paint / Matte Black Baking Paint / Special Paint with Hand-Applied Clear Water Model in Gray / Wood Veneer / White Paint / Wood Flooring / Recessed Lighting

Master Bedroom


The headboard lattice complements the lattice design outside, achieving a cohesive element throughout. The wardrobe is presented in a glass showcase style, utilizing the play of light to create additional layers within the space.
Matte Gray Baking Paint / White Paint / Black Iron Frame Inset with Gray Glass / Wood Veneer / Wood Flooring / Recessed Lighting

Master Bathroom:


Presented in a gray and white color scheme, the use of black frames is an important element for creating a modern feel. The simple and sleek white floor basin, paired with a black-framed splash guard glass, along with black hardware accessories, enhances the overall quality and aesthetic appeal.
Marble / Black Frame Inset with Clear Glass / Wood Veneer / Mirrored Surface
The interior design in Nordic style is characterized by simplicity, natural elements, and brightness, emphasizing the use of wood and natural light. Tailored to the user's preferences, it suits small families of 3-5 people living in urban apartment buildings:

Color Palette and Materials:


The primary choice is the classic color palette of Nordic style, featuring light wood tones, white, and warm hues to create a bright and cozy space. Natural materials are extensively used, with wooden flooring serving as the predominant element throughout the main interior spaces. The living room and dining room feature herringbone-patterned wood flooring, while the bedroom and study utilize lightly colored wooden flooring in a parquet pattern. This method distinguishes between public and private spaces, contributing to the interior's natural ambiance.

Spatial Planning:


The primary public spaces, such as the living and dining areas, are arranged in an open layout. Distinct areas are delineated through wall decorations and materials to define different functional zones. The room adjacent to the living room is planned as a study or office space. The more private spaces on the inner side are designated for the bedrooms of both adults and children.

Furniture Selection:


The furniture is chosen with a style characterized by clean lines, complemented by light or white fabric decorations to create a fresh and comfortable ambiance. The dining table and chairs feature designs incorporating wooden elements, while the lighting fixtures are minimalist and include bamboo, wood, or soft elements, providing a gentle illumination. Warm-toned, lightweight curtains are chosen to maintain an open feel indoors and allow ample natural light to enter the space.
For the soft furnishings, add some Nordic-style art decorations and shelves on the walls, such as abstract paintings, textiles, or handmade crafts, to enhance the artistic atmosphere indoors.
Overall, the interior design prioritizes comfort, functionality, and a natural feel, creating a bright and open sense of space. Tailored to the user's needs, it aims to create a comfortable environment suitable for a small family.
